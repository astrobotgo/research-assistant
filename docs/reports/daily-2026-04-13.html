Galaxies 4 papers

Recent work has delved into understanding the missing ultra-faint satellite galaxies around the Milky Way, suggesting that there are fewer satellites at very low luminosities than expected due to tides and dark matter stripping. Another study explores extreme emission-line galaxies (EELGs) in the local universe, which serve as analogs for reionization-era galaxies, highlighting the importance of high-redshift star formation studies. Furthermore, research on the molecular Kennicutt-Schmidt relation reveals that higher-J CO transitions indicate more linear correlations between gas and star formation rates, suggesting denser gas is directly linked to ongoing star formation. Lastly, [CII] emissions from high-redshift quiescent galaxies provide insights into molecular gas fractions and dust heating mechanisms beyond standard stellar radiation.

Open Questions & Follow-ups

Despite advancements, there remain several open areas. For example, the exact nature and number of ultra-faint dwarf satellites is still unclear and requires more observational data. Additionally, understanding the precise mechanisms driving dust heating in high-redshift quiescent galaxies remains a key challenge. Further work on methodological improvements for analyzing large datasets, such as those from Lyman-alpha forests, will be essential to constrain cosmological parameters accurately.
